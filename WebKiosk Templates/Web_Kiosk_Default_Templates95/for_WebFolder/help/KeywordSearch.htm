Step 1: Find lists of keywords from which to select search terms.
If you are not familiar with the keywords used in the database, just click on the 'Find Keywords' button to bring up a full listing of available keywords. If you would like to be more specific, type a single letter into the box next to the 'find' button to find keywords beginning with that letter only.

Step 2: Create your search
Select a keyword from the 'Keyword Pick List' and click on the 'Add >>' button to add it to the search list on the right. You may add up to 6 terms. To remove a keyword from your search, click on it to highlight it, then click on the '<< Remove' button.

Step 3: Refining your search
If you selected more than one word, you can choose to see results for ALL of these keywords or ANY of them. Note: Selecting ANY will produce more results.

Step 4: Advanced Selection Options
The Advanced Search may be used to refine any selection of object records made active through a previous search or portfolio.
  • To expand the number of records in your selection, choose "Add to Selection."
  • To search only within the active selection, choose "Search Only Current Selection."
  • To ignore records found in previous searches, choose "New Selection."

Step 5: Finish
Click on the Find Objects button to complete your search.
